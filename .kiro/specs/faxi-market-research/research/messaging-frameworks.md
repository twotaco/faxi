# Faxi Messaging Frameworks for Core ICPs

## Document Overview

This document presents comprehensive messaging frameworks for Faxi's three core Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs):
1. Elder-Offline-Fax-Users
2. Online-Families
3. Business Partners

Each messaging framework includes:
- Headline formulas with variables
- Subheadline formulas with variables
- Key messages (3-5 core messages)
- Tone and voice guidance
- Language complexity guidelines
- Visual preferences and design guidance
- Calls-to-action (primary, secondary, tertiary)

**Research Date**: November 27, 2025
**Task**: Task 14 - Create messaging frameworks for core ICPs
**Requirements Addressed**: 6.5, 7.1, 7.2, 7.3
**Based On**: Value propositions (Task 13), ICP research (Tasks 6-8), Cultural analysis (Tasks 2-3)

## 1. Messaging Framework: Elder-Offline-Fax-Users

### Headline Formulas

**Formula 1: Benefit + Familiar Tool**
```
[Action Verb] [Online Service] using your [Familiar Tool]—no [Barrier] needed
```

**Examples**:
- "Shop online using your fax machine—no new technology needed"
- "Book appointments using your fax machine—no computer needed"
- "Access services using your fax machine—no learning needed"
- "Order products using your fax machine—no smartphone needed"

**Formula 2: Independence + Simplicity**
```
[Maintain/Keep] your [Value] with [Simple Solution]
```

**Examples**:
- "Maintain your independence with simple fax-to-internet service"
- "Keep your dignity with technology you already know"
- "Preserve your autonomy with familiar fax access"
- "Stay independent with no new technology to learn"

**Formula 3: Problem Solved**
```
[Online Service] without [Barrier]—just [Simple Action]
```

**Examples**:
- "Online shopping without computers—just send a fax"
- "Internet services without learning—just use your fax machine"
- "Modern convenience without technology anxiety—just fax your request"
- "Digital access without burden—just write and send"


### Subheadline Formulas

**Formula 1: How It Works + Benefit**
```
[Simple Action], and [System Name] [handles complexity]. [Key Benefit]. [Price Point].
```

**Examples**:
- "Send a fax with your request, and Faxi handles the internet. No learning, no burden on family. ¥980 per month."
- "Write your shopping list and fax it, and Faxi orders online for you. Stay independent, no computer needed. Starting at ¥980/month."
- "Fax your appointment request, and Faxi books it online. Maintain your dignity, reduce family burden. Just ¥980/month."

**Formula 2: Three Benefits**
```
[Benefit 1], [Benefit 2], [Benefit 3]—all through your familiar fax machine.
```

**Examples**:
- "No new technology to learn, no burden on your children, no loss of independence—all through your familiar fax machine."
- "Shop online, book appointments, access services—all through the fax machine you already know how to use."
- "Stay independent, reduce family burden, access modern services—all without learning computers or smartphones."

**Formula 3: Contrast + Solution**
```
No [Barrier 1], no [Barrier 2], no [Barrier 3]. Just [Simple Action] and [Benefit].
```

**Examples**:
- "No smartphones, no computers, no apps to download. Just send a fax and access online services."
- "No learning curve, no technology anxiety, no burden on family. Just use your fax machine and stay independent."
- "No new devices, no passwords, no confusion. Just fax your request and get what you need."

"Protected from online scams and fraud, with human support in Japanese whenever you need it. Clear pricing at ¥980-1,980 per month—less than ¥66 per day, with no hidden fees."

### Tone and Voice

**Overall Tone**: Respectful, empowering, warm, reassuring

**Voice Characteristics**:
- **Respectful**: Never patronizing or condescending; treats elderly as capable adults
- **Empowering**: Emphasizes what they CAN do, not what they can't
- **Warm**: Friendly and approachable, like talking to a trusted neighbor
- **Reassuring**: Addresses concerns without creating new anxieties
- **Clear**: Simple and direct without being simplistic
- **Dignified**: Honors their experience and wisdom

**Language Guidelines**:
- Use polite Japanese (丁寧語, teineigo) but not overly formal
- Avoid technical jargon and English loanwords when possible
- Use concrete examples rather than abstract concepts
- Speak in active voice with clear subjects
- Avoid negative framing ("you can't" → "you can")
- Use "you" and "your" to make it personal

**What to Avoid**:
- ❌ Patronizing language ("even you can do it")
- ❌ Emphasizing age or limitations
- ❌ Complex technical explanations
- ❌ Creating anxiety about technology
- ❌ Suggesting they're behind the times
- ❌ Implying they're a burden on family

**What to Emphasize**:
- ✅ Familiarity and comfort
- ✅ Independence and capability
- ✅ Dignity and respect
- ✅ Simplicity and ease
- ✅ Safety and security
- ✅ Affordability and value

### Language Complexity

**Level**: Simple and clear

**Characteristics**:
- Short sentences (10-15 words average)
- Common, everyday vocabulary
- Concrete nouns and active verbs
- Minimal subordinate clauses
- One idea per sentence
- Frequent use of examples

**Sentence Structure Examples**:

**Good**:
- "Send a fax. Faxi handles the internet. You get what you need."
- "Use your fax machine. Access online shopping. Stay independent."
- "No new technology. No learning. Just simple fax service."

**Avoid**:
- "By leveraging your existing fax infrastructure, Faxi facilitates seamless integration with digital services."
- "Our platform enables elderly users to interface with e-commerce ecosystems through familiar analog communication channels."

**Reading Level**: 6th-8th grade equivalent (Japanese)
**Kanji Usage**: Common kanji with furigana for less common characters
**Sentence Length**: 10-20 characters per phrase in Japanese

### Visual Preferences

**Photography Style**:
- Real elderly people (not models), Japanese, 65-85 years old
- Natural settings (home, community center, not studio)
- Showing confidence and capability, not confusion
- Intergenerational scenes showing family harmony
- Close-ups of hands using fax machine (familiar, capable)
- Warm, natural lighting (not harsh or clinical)

**Color Palette**:
- Warm, calming colors (soft blues, greens, earth tones)
- High contrast for readability (dark text on light background)
- Avoid: Bright neon, harsh reds, overly saturated colors
- Traditional Japanese aesthetic elements acceptable

**Typography**:
- Large, clear fonts (minimum 14pt for body, 24pt+ for headlines)
- High legibility fonts (Gothic/sans-serif for Japanese)
- Generous line spacing (1.5-2x)
- Short line lengths (40-60 characters)
- Clear hierarchy with size and weight

**Layout**:
- Clean, uncluttered design
- Generous white space
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Step-by-step visual instructions
- Icons and illustrations to support text
- Avoid: Busy layouts, multiple columns, small text

**Imagery Themes**:
- Independence and dignity
- Family connection and harmony
- Familiar technology (fax machines)
- Everyday activities (shopping, appointments)
- Japanese cultural elements (subtle, not stereotypical)
- Positive emotions (confidence, relief, satisfaction)


### Calls-to-Action (CTAs)

**Primary CTA: Sign Up / Start Service**

**Options**:
1. "無料でお試し" (Try Free) / "Start Free Trial"
   - Best for: Risk-averse users who want to test before committing
   - Context: Homepage, service pages
   - Action: Leads to simple signup form

2. "今すぐ始める" (Start Now) / "Get Started Today"
   - Best for: Users ready to commit after seeing benefits
   - Context: After detailed explanation, pricing page
   - Action: Leads to signup with payment

3. "お申し込み" (Apply/Register)
   - Best for: Formal, traditional preference
   - Context: Traditional media, print materials
   - Action: Leads to signup form or phone number

**Design Guidelines**:
- Large, prominent button (minimum 48px height)
- High contrast color (but not harsh)
- Clear, simple text (3-5 words maximum)
- Include phone number alternative: "または電話で: 0120-XXX-XXX"

**Secondary CTA: Learn More / Get Information**

**Options**:
1. "詳しく見る" (See Details) / "Learn More"
   - Best for: Users who need more information before deciding
   - Context: Homepage, initial touchpoints
   - Action: Leads to service explanation page

2. "使い方を見る" (See How It Works) / "See How It Works"
   - Best for: Users concerned about complexity
   - Context: After benefit statements
   - Action: Leads to step-by-step guide with visuals

3. "資料請求" (Request Materials) / "Request Information"
   - Best for: Traditional users who prefer physical materials
   - Context: Traditional media, community outreach
   - Action: Sends brochure by mail or fax

4. "よくある質問" (FAQ) / "Common Questions"
   - Best for: Users with specific concerns
   - Context: Throughout site, especially near signup
   - Action: Leads to FAQ page

**Design Guidelines**:
- Less prominent than primary CTA (outline button or text link)
- Clear but not competing with primary action
- Positioned near primary CTA for easy access

**Tertiary CTA: Contact / Support**

**Options**:
1. "電話で相談" (Consult by Phone) / "Call Us"
   - Best for: Users who prefer human interaction
   - Context: Throughout site, especially near complex information
   - Action: Displays phone number prominently: "0120-XXX-XXX (通話無料)"

2. "ご家族の方へ" (For Family Members) / "Information for Family"
   - Best for: Elderly users who want family involvement
   - Context: Throughout site
   - Action: Leads to family-focused information page

3. "お問い合わせ" (Inquiries) / "Contact Us"
   - Best for: General questions or concerns
   - Context: Footer, contact page
   - Action: Leads to contact form or displays phone/email

**Design Guidelines**:
- Subtle but always accessible
- Phone number should be clickable on mobile
- Include hours of operation in Japanese time
- Emphasize Japanese language support

### CTA Placement Strategy

**Homepage**:
- Primary CTA: Above fold, after headline/subheadline
- Secondary CTA: After benefits section
- Tertiary CTA: Persistent in header/footer

**Service Page**:
- Primary CTA: After each major benefit section
- Secondary CTA: "See How It Works" early in page
- Tertiary CTA: "Call Us" near pricing information

**Pricing Page**:
- Primary CTA: Next to each pricing tier
- Secondary CTA: "Compare Plans" or "FAQ"
- Tertiary CTA: "Talk to Us" for questions

**Traditional Media (Print, TV)**:
- Primary: Phone number (large, memorable)
- Secondary: Website URL (simple, easy to remember)
- Tertiary: QR code (for family members to scan)

### CTA Copy Testing Recommendations

**Test Variables**:
- Action-oriented vs. benefit-oriented ("Start Now" vs. "Get Independence")
- Formal vs. casual language
- With vs. without price mention
- With vs. without risk reversal ("Free trial", "Cancel anytime")

**Success Metrics**:
- Click-through rate
- Conversion rate
- Phone call volume
- Time to decision

## 2. Messaging Framework: Online-Families

```
Give your [parent/relative] [their benefit] and yourself [your benefit]
```

**Examples**:

- "Give your elderly relatives autonomy and yourself time back"
- "Give your parents dignity and yourself relief from caregiving burden"
- "Give your family members freedom and yourself work-life balance"

**Formula 2: Problem Solved**
```
[Solve parent problem] without [your sacrifice/burden]
```

**Examples**:
- "Enable your parents to access online services without teaching them technology"
- "Help your parents stay independent without constant tech support calls"
- "Connect your parents to modern services without the frustration of explaining"
- "Support your parents' autonomy without sacrificing your time"

**Formula 3: Transformation**
```
From [current pain] to [desired state] in [timeframe/simplicity]
```

**Examples**:
- "From tech support calls to independent parents in minutes"
- "From caregiving burden to family harmony with one simple solution"
- "From constant worry to peace of mind—no teaching required"
- "From geographic distance to connected care with familiar technology"


### Subheadline Formulas

**Formula 1: How + Benefits**
```
Enable them to [parent action] through their familiar fax machine, [your benefit 1] while [your benefit 2].
```

**Examples**:
- "Enable them to access modern services through their familiar fax machine, reducing your caregiving burden while maintaining their dignity."
- "Let them shop online and book appointments through their fax machine, giving you time back while preserving their independence."
- "Help them stay connected through technology they already know, reducing your stress while honoring their autonomy."

**Formula 2: Three Benefits**
```
[Benefit 1], [benefit 2], and [benefit 3]—all without [common barrier].
```

**Examples**:
- "Reduce tech support calls, save hours each week, and improve family harmony—all without teaching them new technology."
- "Enable their independence, reduce your worry, and reclaim your time—all through their familiar fax machine."
- "Bridge the distance, reduce the burden, and maintain their dignity—all with one simple solution."

**Formula 3: Problem + Solution + Outcome**
```
Your parents [problem]. Faxi [solution]. You get [outcome].
```

**Examples**:
- "Your parents can't use smartphones. Faxi uses their fax machine. You get peace of mind and time back."
- "Your parents need help with online services. Faxi provides access through fax. You reduce caregiving burden by 40-60%."
- "Your parents feel isolated from digital world. Faxi bridges the gap. You fulfill filial duty without sacrifice."

### Key Messages

**Message 1: Reduce Caregiving Burden and Reclaim Your Time**
"Stop being the tech support hotline for your parents. Faxi enables them to handle online shopping, appointments, and services independently—saving you 5-10 hours per week and reducing stress."

**Supporting Points**:
- No more emergency "how do I..." phone calls
- No more weekend trips to help with errands
- No more explaining the same thing repeatedly
- Reclaim time for your own work and family
- Reduce risk of having to leave job for caregiving (99,000 annually do)

**Message 2: Bridge Geographic Distance**
"Whether you live 15 minutes or 3 hours away, enable your parents to access services independently. No need to rush over for every appointment or shopping trip."

**Supporting Points**:
- 50% of adult children live 15+ minutes from parents
- 27.6% live in different prefectures
- Distance no longer a barrier to parents' independence
- Reduce guilt about not being physically present
- Maintain connection without constant logistics

**Message 3: Peace of Mind Without Constant Monitoring**
"Know your parents can access essential services even when you're not available. Reduce worry while respecting their privacy and autonomy."

**Supporting Points**:
- Parents can handle emergencies and daily needs
- Optional activity monitoring (with permission) for safety
- Reduce anxiety about parents being "cut off"
- Better work-life balance with less interruption
- 67.3% of caregivers report emotional stress—reduce yours

**Message 4: Fulfill Filial Duty Through Smart Solution**
"Honor your responsibility to care for parents (孝行, kōkō) through enabling technology, not just personal sacrifice. Provide comprehensive support while maintaining family harmony."

**Supporting Points**:
- Culturally appropriate way to fulfill caregiving obligation
- Reduce guilt about not doing enough (54.6% feel this)
- Improve communication quality (less logistics, more connection)
- Demonstrate care through thoughtful solution
- Reduce family conflict about care responsibilities

**Message 5: No Teaching Required—Works Immediately**
"Your parents already know how to use a fax machine. No classes, no explanations, no frustration. They start using it immediately, and you never have to troubleshoot."

**Supporting Points**:
- Zero learning curve for parents
- No repeated questions or forgotten instructions
- No frustration from teaching attempts (42.3% struggle with this)
- Works for parents unable or unwilling to learn new technology
- 76% of elderly have technology anxiety—Faxi eliminates it

### Tone and Voice

**Overall Tone**: Empathetic, practical, solution-focused, respectful

**Voice Characteristics**:
- **Empathetic**: Acknowledges the real challenges of caregiving
- **Practical**: Focuses on concrete benefits and time savings
- **Solution-focused**: Emphasizes what can be improved, not just problems
- **Respectful**: Honors both your needs and parents' dignity
- **Reassuring**: Reduces guilt and worry
- **Efficient**: Values your time with clear, concise information

**Language Guidelines**:
- Acknowledge caregiving challenges without being negative
- Use "you" and "your parents" to make it personal
- Quantify benefits when possible (hours saved, stress reduced)
- Balance parent benefits with your benefits
- Avoid making parents sound incapable or burdensome
- Emphasize win-win outcomes for both generations

**What to Avoid**:
- ❌ Making parents sound like a burden
- ❌ Suggesting you're a bad child for not doing more
- ❌ Overpromising ("solves all problems")
- ❌ Dismissing the difficulty of caregiving
- ❌ Technical jargon about how it works
- ❌ Guilt-tripping about filial duty

**What to Emphasize**:
- ✅ Time savings and stress reduction
- ✅ Parents' independence and dignity
- ✅ Practical, immediate benefits
- ✅ Work-life balance improvement
- ✅ Family harmony and connection
- ✅ Smart solution vs. personal sacrifice

## 3. Messaging Framework: Business Partners

### Headline Formulas

**Formula 1: Market Opportunity + Scale**
```
Access [number] [customer segment] through their familiar [interface]
```

**Examples**:
- "Access 6-7.5 million offline elderly customers through their familiar fax interface"
- "Reach 14.9 million internet-excluded elderly through proven fax technology"
- "Tap into ¥9.2 trillion elderly e-commerce opportunity through fax-to-internet bridge"
- "Connect with 40.5% of elderly market currently excluded from your digital services"

**Formula 2: Benefit + Without Barrier**
```
[Business Outcome] without [Customer Barrier]
```

**Examples**:
- "Expand your elderly customer base without requiring them to change behavior"
- "Increase revenue from elderly segment without digital literacy barriers"
- "Improve accessibility compliance without complex technology adoption"
- "Reduce customer acquisition costs without sacrificing market reach"

**Formula 3: Problem Solved + ROI**
```
[Solve business problem] and [achieve outcome] in [timeframe]
```

**Examples**:
- "Access untapped elderly market and achieve ROI in 6-18 months"
- "Reduce no-show rates and increase appointment volume within 90 days"
- "Expand market reach and improve compliance in one integrated solution"
- "Lower customer service costs and grow revenue simultaneously"


### Subheadline Formulas

**Formula 1: How + Benefits + ROI**
```
[Action/Integration], [benefit 1], and [benefit 2] without [barrier]—[ROI timeframe].
```

**Examples**:
- "Integrate with Faxi's fax-to-internet platform, expand your addressable market by 40.5%, and improve accessibility compliance without requiring customers to change behavior—achieve ROI in 6-18 months."
- "Connect your services to 6-7.5 million offline elderly customers, reduce customer acquisition costs by 30-50%, and differentiate from competitors without complex technology adoption—measurable results within 90 days."
- "Enable elderly customers to access your services through familiar fax interface, increase revenue from underserved segment, and reduce customer service burden by 40-60%—positive cash flow in first year."

**Formula 2: Three Business Outcomes**
```
[Outcome 1], [outcome 2], [outcome 3]—all through [solution].
```

**Examples**:
- "Expand market reach, increase revenue, improve compliance—all through one integrated fax-to-internet platform."
- "Reduce CAC, grow customer base, differentiate from competitors—all without requiring customer behavior change."
- "Access untapped market, lower service costs, enhance brand reputation—all with measurable ROI."

**Formula 3: Market Data + Solution**
```
[Market statistic]. [Your current limitation]. Faxi [solution and outcome].
```

**Examples**:
- "40.5% of elderly (14.9 million) don't use internet services. Your digital-only strategy excludes them. Faxi bridges the gap and delivers them to your platform."
- "62.3% of hospital patients are elderly, but only 12.3% use online appointments. Faxi enables the other 50% to book through familiar fax interface."
- "¥22.7 trillion e-commerce market, but 67.3% of elderly don't shop online. Faxi unlocks ¥9.2 trillion opportunity for your platform."

### Key Messages

**Message 1: Access Massive Untapped Market**
"Reach 6-7.5 million elderly customers who currently cannot use your online services. Expand your addressable market by 40.5% of the elderly population—a ¥9.2 trillion opportunity in e-commerce alone."

**Supporting Points**:
- 14.9 million elderly have low/no internet usage
- 14.2 million households own fax machines
- 8.6 million use fax regularly (proven comfort)
- 62.3% of healthcare patients are elderly
- 67.3% of elderly don't shop online (24.7 million people)
- Elderly have ¥3.18 million average household income (spending power)

**Message 2: Measurable ROI and Revenue Growth**
"Generate incremental revenue from previously inaccessible segment with 6-18 month ROI. Reduce customer acquisition costs by 30-50% while expanding market reach."

**Supporting Points**:

*Healthcare*:
- Reduce 8.7% no-show rate (save ¥15,000-30,000 per missed appointment)
- Increase appointment volume through easier booking
- Average elderly patient: 14.2 visits/year (vs 5.8 for under-65)
- Estimated revenue increase: 15-25% from elderly segment

*E-Commerce*:
- Average order ¥8,000, frequency 2-4 times/month
- Even 1% penetration = ¥593 million/month market
- High-value categories: health/wellness (34.7%), food (18.2%)
- Lower CAC than traditional elderly marketing (TV, print)

*Government*:
- Reduce in-person service costs: ¥3,000-5,000 per transaction
- Reduce call center volume: 30-40% are elderly needing help
- Estimated savings: ¥500-2,000 per elderly citizen annually

**Message 3: Reduce Customer Service Burden and Costs**
"Decrease support costs by 40-60% through self-service option via familiar interface. Scale your elderly customer base without proportional staff increase."

**Supporting Points**:
- 30-40% of healthcare staff time on phone appointments
- Elderly customers require 3-5x more support time (e-commerce)
- Phone support not scalable
- Faxi AI handles routine requests
- Human support only for exceptions
- Estimated cost: ¥2,000-4,000 per phone interaction reduced

**Message 4: Improve Accessibility Compliance and Brand Reputation**
"Meet regulatory requirements and demonstrate social responsibility. Differentiate through inclusive service design while avoiding compliance penalties."

**Supporting Points**:
- Barrier-Free Act amendments increasing requirements

- Government procurement requires accessibility compliance
- Positive PR and media coverage opportunity
- CSR credentials and employee pride
- Customer loyalty from inclusive approach

**Message 5: Competitive Differentiation and First-Mover Advantage**
"Stand out from competitors through unique channel and inclusive approach. Capture market share before competitors can replicate."

**Supporting Points**:
- Most competitors ignore offline elderly segment

### Tone and Voice

**Overall Tone**: Professional, data-driven, results-oriented, confident

**Voice Characteristics**:
- **Professional**: Business-appropriate language and formality
- **Data-driven**: Backed by statistics and market research
- **Results-oriented**: Focuses on ROI and business outcomes
- **Confident**: Demonstrates expertise and market knowledge
- **Consultative**: Partner approach, not just vendor
- **Strategic**: Emphasizes long-term competitive advantage

**Language Guidelines**:
- Use business terminology and metrics (CAC, LTV, ROI, market penetration)
- Lead with data and quantifiable benefits
- Speak to decision-makers (executives, managers, procurement)
- Balance opportunity with practical implementation
- Use industry-specific language for each partner type
- Emphasize mutual benefit and partnership

**What to Avoid**:
- ❌ Overly technical jargon about how Faxi works
- ❌ Focusing on elderly needs without business benefits
- ❌ Vague claims without data support
- ❌ Underselling the market opportunity
- ❌ Ignoring implementation concerns
- ❌ One-size-fits-all messaging (customize by industry)

**What to Emphasize**:
- ✅ Market size and revenue opportunity
- ✅ ROI and payback period
- ✅ Competitive advantage
- ✅ Scalability and efficiency
- ✅ Compliance and risk mitigation
- ✅ Measurable outcomes and KPIs


### Language Complexity

**Level**: Technical and professional

**Characteristics**:
- Longer, complex sentences (20-35 words)
- Business and technical vocabulary
- Industry-specific terminology
- Data-heavy with statistics and projections
- Multiple ideas connected with sophisticated logic
- Assumes business literacy

**Sentence Structure Examples**:

**Good**:
- "By integrating Faxi's fax-to-internet platform, healthcare providers can reduce their 8.7% no-show rate while increasing appointment volume from the 62.3% of patients who are elderly, achieving ROI within 6-12 months through reduced administrative costs and increased revenue per patient."
- "E-commerce platforms can access the 67.3% of elderly (24.7 million people) who don't currently shop online, representing a ¥9.2 trillion market opportunity, with customer acquisition costs 30-50% lower than traditional elderly marketing channels."
- "Government agencies can meet Digital Agency accessibility mandates while reducing in-person service costs by ¥3,000-5,000 per transaction, achieving both compliance and operational efficiency through a single integrated solution."

**Avoid**:
- Too simple: "Faxi helps you reach elderly customers. They use fax. You make money."
- Too complex: "Leveraging synergistic paradigms in analog-digital convergence, Faxi facilitates omnichannel ecosystem optimization through legacy infrastructure monetization strategies."

**Reading Level**: College/professional level
**Information Density**: High (include detailed data, but organize clearly)
**Tone**: Executive briefing style—comprehensive but efficient
