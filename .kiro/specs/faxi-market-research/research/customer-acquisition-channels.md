# Customer Acquisition Channels Analysis for Core ICPs

## Document Overview

This document presents comprehensive customer acquisition channel analysis for Faxi's three core Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs):
1. Elder-Offline-Fax-Users
2. Online-Families
3. Business Partners

Each channel analysis includes:
- Channel identification and description
- Reach estimation (potential audience size)
- Cost estimation (CAC and ongoing costs)
- Conversion rate estimates
- Priority ranking (High/Medium/Low)
- Channel strategy and tactics
- Budget allocation recommendations
- Success metrics and KPIs

**Research Date**: November 27, 2025
**Task**: Task 15 - Analyze customer acquisition channels for core ICPs
**Requirements Addressed**: 10.1, 10.2, 10.3, 10.5
**Based On**: ICP research (Tasks 6-8), Demographics (Task 2), Family dynamics (Task 3), Messaging frameworks (Task 14)

## Executive Summary

### Key Findings

**Elder-Offline-Fax-Users**: Traditional, offline channels dominate with community-based approaches showing highest conversion potential. Direct mail and community partnerships offer best ROI.

**Online-Families**: Digital channels excel with content marketing and targeted advertising showing strong performance. SEO and social media provide scalable acquisition.

**Business Partners**: Relationship-driven channels critical with direct sales and industry events providing highest value deals. Long sales cycles require multi-touch approach.

**Year 1 Total Budget**: ¥50 million ($333,000 USD)

**By ICP**:
- Elder-Offline-Fax-Users: ¥20 million (40%)

**By Channel Type**:

## 1. Customer Acquisition Channels: Elder-Offline-Fax-Users

### Target Audience Profile

**Demographics**:
- Age: 70-85+ years (primary focus on 75+)
- Gender: 56.3% female, 43.7% male
- Location: 68.4% urban, 31.6% rural
- Income: ¥2-4 million/year (35.7% of segment)
- Technology: 38.7% own fax machines, 76% have technology anxiety

**Psychographics**:
- Value independence and dignity
- Prefer familiar, traditional methods
- Trust community recommendations
- Skeptical of new technology
- Influenced by family members

**Market Size**: 6.2-7.5 million potential customers

**Source**: Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications, "Communications Usage Trend Survey 2023", August 2023; Statistics Bureau of Japan, "Population Estimates 2023", October 2023

### Channel 1.1: Direct Mail (ダイレクトメール)

**Description**: Physical mail sent to elderly households with fax machines, including brochures, introductory offers, and simple instructions.

**Reach Estimation**:
- **Addressable Market**: 14.2 million fax-owning households
- **Target Segment**: 6-7.5 million elderly with low internet usage
- **Campaign Reach**: 100,000-500,000 households per campaign
- **Geographic Focus**: Urban areas first (higher density), then rural

**Cost Estimation**:
- **Production Cost**: ¥50-80 per piece (design, printing, materials)
- **Postage**: ¥84-120 per piece (standard mail)
- **Total Cost Per Piece**: ¥134-200
- **Campaign Cost**: ¥13.4-20 million per 100,000 pieces
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: ¥8,000-15,000
  - Assumes 1-2% response rate
  - Assumes 50-70% conversion of responders

**Conversion Estimation**:
- **Response Rate**: 1-2% (typical for elderly direct mail in Japan)
- **Conversion Rate**: 50-70% of responders (high intent)
- **Overall Conversion**: 0.5-1.4% of mail recipients
- **Expected Customers**: 500-1,400 per 100,000 pieces

**Priority**: **HIGH**

**Rationale**:
- Elderly trust physical mail (familiar, tangible)
- Can include visual instructions and examples
- No technology barrier to receive information
- High-quality targeting possible (fax ownership data)
- Proven channel for elderly in Japan

**Channel Strategy**:

1. **Targeting**:
   - Purchase/rent mailing lists of fax-owning households
   - Focus on 70+ age demographic
   - Geographic segmentation (urban first, then rural)
   - Income targeting (¥2-6 million households)

2. **Creative Approach**:
   - Large, clear typography (minimum 14pt)
   - Step-by-step visual instructions
   - Real elderly testimonials with photos
   - Emphasis on "no new technology" and "use your fax"
   - Include actual-size fax form example
   - Warm, respectful imagery

3. **Offer Structure**:
   - Free trial (30 days, no credit card required)
   - Introductory pricing (¥500/month for first 3 months)
   - Money-back guarantee
   - Free setup assistance by phone
   - "Bring a friend" referral incentive

4. **Response Mechanisms**:
   - Toll-free phone number (prominent, large font)
   - Fax-back response form (ironic but effective)
   - Prepaid return postcard
   - QR code (for family members who may see mail)

5. **Follow-Up**:
   - Phone call to responders within 24 hours
   - Welcome kit sent by mail
   - Personal onboarding call
   - Check-in at 1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month

**Tactics**:
- Test multiple creative variations (A/B testing)
- Seasonal campaigns (New Year, Respect for the Aged Day)
- Partner with Japan Post for targeting assistance
- Include postage-paid return envelope
- Use testimonials from similar demographic

**Success Metrics**:
- Response rate (target: 1.5%+)
- Conversion rate (target: 60%+)
- CAC (target: under ¥12,000)
- Customer lifetime value (LTV)
- LTV:CAC ratio (target: 10:1+)
- 90-day retention rate

**Budget Allocation**: ¥8 million (40% of Elder-Offline budget)

### Channel 1.2: Community Organizations and Senior Centers (地域組織・老人センター)

**Description**: Partnerships with community centers, senior citizen clubs, neighborhood associations, and social welfare organizations to reach elderly through trusted community channels.

**Reach Estimation**:
- **Senior Centers**: 4,500+ facilities nationwide
- **Neighborhood Associations**: 300,000+ (chonaikai/jichikai)
- **Senior Clubs**: 100,000+ (rojin kurabu)
- **Average Members**: 50-200 per organization
- **Total Reach**: 5-10 million elderly through community networks

**Cost Estimation**:
- **Partnership Development**: ¥100,000-500,000 per major organization
- **Materials/Demonstrations**: ¥50,000-100,000 per event
- **Staff Time**: ¥200,000-400,000 per month (community liaison)
- **Incentives**: ¥5,000-10,000 per referral to organization
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: ¥5,000-10,000
  - Lower than direct mail due to trust factor
  - Higher conversion from warm introductions

**Conversion Estimation**:
- **Attendance Rate**: 10-20% of members attend info sessions
- **Conversion Rate**: 15-30% of attendees (high trust)
- **Overall Conversion**: 1.5-6% of organization members
- **Referral Rate**: 20-30% of customers refer others

**Priority**: **HIGH**

**Rationale**:
- High trust in community organizations
- Peer influence and social proof
- Ability to demonstrate in person
- Word-of-mouth amplification
- Cultural importance of community (地域社会)
- Lower CAC than paid advertising

**Channel Strategy**:

1. **Partnership Development**:
   - Identify key community organizations by region
   - Approach through formal introduction (紹介)
   - Offer value to organization (member benefit, commission)
   - Provide training materials for staff
   - Create co-branded materials

2. **Engagement Tactics**:
   - Monthly information sessions at senior centers
   - Demonstration booths at community events
   - "Lunch and learn" presentations
   - Hands-on fax machine demonstrations
   - Success story sharing sessions

3. **Materials and Support**:
   - Large-print brochures and posters
   - Demonstration fax machines
   - Video testimonials from similar users
   - Simple FAQ handouts
   - Staff training on how to explain Faxi

4. **Incentive Structure**:
   - Organization receives ¥2,000 per signup
   - Member discount (¥200/month off)
   - Group rates for 10+ members
   - Free trial extended to 60 days for community members
   - Referral bonuses (¥1,000 per successful referral)

5. **Community Ambassador Program**:
   - Recruit satisfied customers as ambassadors
   - Provide ambassador training and materials
   - Compensate with free service or cash (¥5,000/month)
   - Enable peer-to-peer education
   - Create local success stories

**Tactics**:
- Start with pilot in 2-3 cities
- Document success stories and testimonials
- Create replicable playbook for scaling
- Leverage Respect for the Aged Day (9/15) for campaigns
- Partner with social welfare councils (社会福祉協議会)
- Engage with silver human resource centers

**Success Metrics**:
- Number of partner organizations (target: 100 in year 1)
- Attendance at information sessions
- Conversion rate from sessions (target: 20%+)
- CAC (target: under ¥8,000)
- Referral rate (target: 25%+)
- Customer satisfaction scores
- Retention rate (target: 85%+)

**Budget Allocation**: ¥6 million (30% of Elder-Offline budget)

### Channel 1.3: Local Newspaper Advertising (地方新聞広告)

**Description**: Print advertisements in local and regional newspapers that elderly readers trust and regularly consume.

**Reach Estimation**:
- **Newspaper Readership 65+**: 68.7% read newspapers daily
- **Major Regional Papers**: Yomiuri (9M), Asahi (5.2M), Mainichi (2.4M)
- **Local Papers**: 100+ regional newspapers
- **Elderly Readership**: 15-20 million elderly read newspapers regularly
- **Ad Reach**: 500,000-2 million per campaign (depending on publication)

**Cost Estimation**:
- **Full Page Ad**: ¥2-8 million (national), ¥500,000-2 million (regional)
- **Half Page Ad**: ¥1-4 million (national), ¥250,000-1 million (regional)
- **Quarter Page Ad**: ¥500,000-2 million (national), ¥125,000-500,000 (regional)
- **Production Cost**: ¥200,000-500,000 per creative
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: ¥10,000-20,000
  - Lower response rates than direct mail
  - But broader reach and brand building

**Conversion Estimation**:
- **Response Rate**: 0.1-0.3% (typical for print ads)
- **Conversion Rate**: 40-60% of responders
- **Overall Conversion**: 0.04-0.18% of readers
- **Expected Customers**: 200-900 per 500,000 circulation

**Priority**: **MEDIUM**

**Rationale**:
- High newspaper readership among elderly (68.7%)
- Trust in print media
- Brand building and awareness
- Reaches decision-makers and influencers (family)
- Can target specific regions
- However: expensive, lower direct response than direct mail

**Channel Strategy**:

1. **Publication Selection**:
   - Start with regional papers (better ROI than national)
   - Focus on areas with high elderly population
   - Test multiple publications
   - Consider senior-focused sections/supplements

2. **Ad Placement**:
   - Health/lifestyle sections (high elderly readership)
   - Weekend editions (more time to read)
   - Avoid business/sports sections
   - Consider advertorials (editorial-style ads)

3. **Creative Approach**:
   - Large, clear headline (minimum 24pt)
   - Simple, uncluttered layout
   - Real elderly person in photo (relatable)
   - Prominent phone number
   - Clear value proposition
   - Testimonial or success story

4. **Response Mechanisms**:
   - Toll-free phone number (very prominent)
   - Website URL (for family members)
   - QR code (for family members)
   - Coupon code for tracking

5. **Frequency**:
   - Monthly ads in selected publications
   - Seasonal campaigns (New Year, Respect for the Aged Day)
   - Test frequency vs. reach trade-offs

**Tactics**:
- Start with quarter-page ads to test
- A/B test different headlines and offers
- Track response by publication and placement
- Negotiate bulk rates for multiple insertions
- Consider newspaper insert (flyer) as alternative
- Partner with newspapers for content marketing

**Success Metrics**:
- Response rate by publication (target: 0.2%+)
- Conversion rate (target: 50%+)
- CAC by publication (target: under ¥15,000)
- Brand awareness lift
- Assisted conversions (saw ad, signed up later)

**Budget Allocation**: ¥3 million (15% of Elder-Offline budget)

### Channel 1.4: Local Radio Advertising (地方ラジオ広告)

**Description**: Radio advertisements on stations with high elderly listenership, particularly during daytime programming.

**Reach Estimation**:
- **Radio Listenership 65+**: 42.3% listen to radio regularly
- **Daytime Listeners**: Primarily elderly and homemakers
- **Local Stations**: 100+ AM/FM stations nationwide
- **Average Station Reach**: 50,000-500,000 listeners
- **Elderly Reach**: 20,000-200,000 per station

**Cost Estimation**:
- **30-Second Spot**: ¥10,000-50,000 (local), ¥100,000-500,000 (major city)
- **60-Second Spot**: ¥15,000-75,000 (local), ¥150,000-750,000 (major city)
- **Production Cost**: ¥100,000-300,000 per creative
- **Campaign Cost**: ¥1-3 million per month (20-30 spots/week)
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: ¥8,000-15,000

**Conversion Estimation**:
- **Response Rate**: 0.05-0.15% of listeners
- **Conversion Rate**: 50-70% of responders
- **Overall Conversion**: 0.025-0.105% of listeners
- **Expected Customers**: 5-21 per 20,000 listeners per campaign

**Priority**: **MEDIUM**

**Rationale**:
- Elderly listen to radio (42.3%)
- Daytime programming reaches target demographic
- Audio format accessible (no reading required)
- Can create emotional connection through voice
- Lower cost than TV
- However: harder to convey complex information, no visual

**Channel Strategy**:

1. **Station Selection**:
   - AM stations (higher elderly listenership)
   - Talk radio and news programs
   - Enka music programs (traditional Japanese music)
   - Health and lifestyle shows
   - Local community stations

2. **Timing**:
   - Daytime hours (9am-3pm) - highest elderly listenership
   - Morning news programs (6-9am)
   - Avoid evening/night (lower elderly listening)
   - Consistent schedule (same time/day for recognition)

3. **Creative Approach**:
   - Warm, trustworthy voice (middle-aged to elderly)
   - Simple, clear message (one main point)
   - Repetition of phone number (3-4 times)
   - Testimonial format (real customer story)
   - Emphasize "no new technology" and "use your fax"
   - Include memorable tagline

4. **Response Mechanisms**:
   - Easy-to-remember phone number (0120-XXX-XXX)
   - Repeat phone number multiple times
   - Mention website for family members
   - Offer free information kit by phone

5. **Frequency**:
   - High frequency (20-30 spots per week minimum)
   - Consistent schedule for habit formation
   - 4-8 week campaigns for impact

**Tactics**:
- Test multiple stations and time slots
- Use tracking phone numbers by station
- Consider sponsorship of popular elderly programs
- Explore radio personality endorsements
- Create multiple creative versions to avoid fatigue
- Combine with other channels for reinforcement

**Success Metrics**:
- Call volume by station and time slot
- Conversion rate from calls (target: 60%+)
- CAC by station (target: under ¥12,000)
- Brand recall (survey elderly listeners)
- Assisted conversions

**Budget Allocation**: ¥2 million (10% of Elder-Offline budget)

### Channel 1.5: Healthcare Provider Partnerships (医療機関提携)

**Description**: Partnerships with hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies to reach elderly patients through trusted healthcare touchpoints.

**Reach Estimation**:
- **Hospitals**: 8,300+ nationwide
- **Clinics**: 102,000+ nationwide
- **Pharmacies**: 60,000+ nationwide
- **Elderly Patients**: 62.3% of hospital admissions, 54.7% of outpatient visits
- **Annual Elderly Visits**: 14.2 visits/year average
- **Potential Reach**: 10+ million elderly through healthcare system

**Cost Estimation**:
- **Partnership Fee**: ¥50,000-200,000 per facility per year
- **Materials**: ¥20,000-50,000 per facility (brochures, posters)
- **Commission**: ¥1,000-3,000 per signup
- **Staff Training**: ¥50,000-100,000 per facility
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: ¥4,000-8,000
  - High trust environment = higher conversion
  - Warm referral from healthcare provider

**Conversion Estimation**:
- **Exposure Rate**: 20-40% of patients see materials
- **Interest Rate**: 5-10% request information
- **Conversion Rate**: 30-50% of interested patients
- **Overall Conversion**: 0.3-2% of elderly patients
- **Expected Customers**: 30-200 per facility per year

**Priority**: **HIGH**

**Rationale**:
- Extremely high trust in healthcare providers
- Captive audience (waiting rooms)
- Relevant context (health, independence, aging)
- Elderly visit healthcare frequently (14.2 times/year)
- Natural fit with appointment booking feature
- Low CAC due to trust factor

**Channel Strategy**:

1. **Partnership Development**:
   - Target geriatric clinics and hospitals first
   - Approach through medical associations
   - Offer value to healthcare providers (reduce no-shows, improve patient communication)
   - Provide staff training on Faxi benefits
   - Create co-branded materials

2. **Placement Tactics**:
   - Brochure displays in waiting rooms
   - Posters in examination rooms
   - Information cards with discharge papers
   - Pharmacy bag inserts
   - Doctor/nurse recommendations

3. **Materials**:
   - Large-print brochures focused on health benefits
   - Posters emphasizing appointment booking
   - Success stories from elderly patients
   - Simple FAQ sheets
   - Tear-off cards with phone number

4. **Incentive Structure**:
   - Healthcare provider receives ¥2,000 per signup
   - Patient discount (first month free)
   - Priority appointment booking for Faxi users
   - Reduced no-show rates benefit provider

5. **Integration Opportunities**:
   - Enable appointment booking through Faxi
   - Prescription refill requests via fax
   - Test result notifications via fax
   - Health reminders and follow-ups

**Tactics**:
- Start with pilot partnerships in 2-3 cities
- Document impact on no-show rates and patient satisfaction
- Create case studies for provider recruitment
- Leverage medical associations for introductions
- Attend healthcare conferences
- Offer free trial for healthcare staff's elderly relatives

**Success Metrics**:
- Number of partner facilities (target: 50 in year 1)
- Signups per facility per month
- Conversion rate from materials (target: 1%+)
- CAC (target: under ¥6,000)
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Provider satisfaction and retention

**Budget Allocation**: ¥1 million (5% of Elder-Offline budget)

## 2. Customer Acquisition Channels: Online-Families

### Target Audience Profile

**Demographics**:
- Age: 40-59 years (primary), 60-69 (secondary)
- Gender: 68.4% female (primary caregivers)
- Location: Urban areas (children in cities, parents in rural/suburban)
- Income: ¥4-8 million/year household income
- Technology: 92-97% internet usage, 87-95% smartphone ownership

**Psychographics**:
- Stressed by caregiving burden (67.3%)
- Feel guilty about not doing enough (54.6%)
- Time-constrained (52.3% work-life balance issues)
- Digitally savvy and comfortable with online research
- Influenced by social proof and testimonials
- Value practical solutions and time savings

**Market Size**: 15-20 million adult children of elderly parents

**Source**: Japan Institute for Labour Policy and Training, "Survey on Work-Life Balance and Caregiving 2022", November 2022; Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications, "Communications Usage Trend Survey 2023", August 2023

### Channel 2.1: Search Engine Optimization (SEO) and Content Marketing

**Description**: Organic search traffic through high-quality content targeting caregiving, elderly care, and technology solution keywords.

**Reach Estimation**:
- **Monthly Searches**: 500,000+ for elderly care keywords
- **Target Keywords**: "elderly parent help", "fax service", "elderly technology", "caregiving solutions"
- **Potential Monthly Traffic**: 10,000-50,000 visitors (with strong SEO)
- **Addressable Audience**: Millions searching for elderly care solutions

**Cost Estimation**:
- **Content Creation**: ¥200,000-500,000 per month (writers, editors)
- **SEO Tools**: ¥50,000-100,000 per month
- **Technical SEO**: ¥300,000-500,000 (one-time setup)
- **Link Building**: ¥100,000-300,000 per month
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: ¥3,000-8,000
  - Low ongoing cost after initial investment
  - Compounds over time as content library grows

**Conversion Estimation**:
- **Organic Traffic**: 10,000-50,000 visitors per month (mature site)
- **Conversion Rate**: 2-5% (high-intent traffic)
- **Expected Customers**: 200-2,500 per month (mature)
- **Time to Maturity**: 6-12 months

**Priority**: **HIGH**

**Rationale**:
- Adult children actively search for elderly care solutions
- High-intent traffic (problem-aware)
- Scalable and compounds over time
- Low ongoing CAC after initial investment
- Builds brand authority and trust
- Supports all other channels (research destination)

**Channel Strategy**:

1. **Keyword Strategy**:
   - **Primary Keywords**: "elderly parent technology help", "fax service for elderly", "elderly online shopping assistance"
   - **Long-Tail Keywords**: "how to help elderly parent shop online", "elderly parent can't use smartphone", "caregiving technology solutions Japan"
   - **Local Keywords**: "elderly care Tokyo", "senior services Osaka"
   - **Question Keywords**: "how to help elderly parent with technology", "what services for elderly without internet"

2. **Content Pillars**:
   - **Caregiving Guides**: Comprehensive guides on helping elderly parents
   - **Technology Solutions**: Reviews and comparisons of elderly-friendly tech
   - **Success Stories**: Case studies and testimonials from families
   - **Practical Tips**: How-to articles on specific caregiving challenges
   - **Resource Library**: Downloadable guides, checklists, templates

3. **Content Types**:
   - Blog posts (2-4 per week)
   - Comprehensive guides (2,000-5,000 words)
   - Video content (demonstrations, testimonials)
   - Infographics (shareable, visual)
   - Downloadable resources (lead magnets)
   - FAQ pages (target question keywords)

4. **Technical SEO**:
   - Fast page load times
   - Mobile optimization (critical for this audience)
   - Structured data markup
   - Internal linking strategy
   - XML sitemap and robots.txt
   - HTTPS and security

5. **Link Building**:
   - Guest posts on caregiving blogs
   - Partnerships with elderly care resources
   - Digital PR and media outreach
   - Resource page link building
   - Broken link building
   - HARO (Help a Reporter Out) responses

**Tactics**:
- Conduct comprehensive keyword research
- Create content calendar (3 months ahead)
- Optimize existing pages for target keywords
- Build topic clusters around main themes
- Monitor rankings and adjust strategy
- Repurpose content across formats
- Update and refresh top-performing content

**Success Metrics**:
- Organic traffic growth (target: 20% month-over-month)
- Keyword rankings (target: top 10 for 50+ keywords)
- Conversion rate from organic (target: 3%+)
- CAC from organic (target: under ¥5,000)
- Content engagement (time on page, bounce rate)
- Backlink growth

**Budget Allocation**: ¥6 million (30% of Online-Families budget)

### Channel 2.2: Google Ads and Search Engine Marketing (SEM)

**Description**: Paid search advertising targeting high-intent keywords related to elderly care, caregiving, and technology solutions.

**Reach Estimation**:
- **Monthly Searches**: 500,000+ for target keywords
- **Ad Impressions**: 100,000-500,000 per month
- **Click-Through Rate**: 3-8% (well-optimized ads)
- **Monthly Clicks**: 3,000-40,000

**Cost Estimation**:
- **Cost Per Click (CPC)**: ¥100-500 (depending on keyword competitiveness)
- **Monthly Ad Spend**: ¥2-5 million
- **Management Fee**: ¥200,000-500,000 per month (agency or in-house)
- **Creative Development**: ¥100,000-300,000 per month
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: ¥5,000-12,000
  - Higher than SEO but immediate results
  - Highly scalable with budget

**Conversion Estimation**:
- **Monthly Clicks**: 10,000-20,000 (¥3M budget)
- **Landing Page Conversion**: 5-10%
- **Expected Customers**: 500-2,000 per month
- **Immediate Results**: Traffic starts day 1

**Priority**: **HIGH**

**Rationale**:
- Immediate traffic and results
- Highly targetable (keywords, demographics, location)
- Scalable with budget
- Measurable ROI
- Complements SEO efforts
- Captures high-intent searchers

**Channel Strategy**:

1. **Campaign Structure**:
   - **Brand Campaign**: Faxi-related keywords (defensive)
   - **Problem-Aware Campaign**: "elderly parent help", "caregiving solutions"
   - **Solution-Aware Campaign**: "fax service", "elderly technology"
   - **Competitor Campaign**: Competitor brand keywords
   - **Remarketing Campaign**: Previous website visitors

2. **Keyword Strategy**:
   - **High-Intent Keywords**: "elderly fax service", "help elderly parent online"
   - **Problem Keywords**: "elderly parent can't use smartphone", "caregiving burden"
   - **Comparison Keywords**: "elderly technology solutions comparison"
   - **Negative Keywords**: Exclude irrelevant searches (jobs, medical conditions)

3. **Ad Copy Approach**:
   - Headline: Address pain point or benefit
   - Description: Solution and unique value
   - Call-to-action: "Start Free Trial", "Learn More"
   - Ad extensions: Sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets
   - Emotional appeal: "Give your parents independence"

4. **Landing Page Strategy**:
   - Dedicated landing pages for each campaign
   - Match ad message to landing page headline
   - Clear value proposition above fold
   - Social proof (testimonials, reviews)
   - Simple conversion form
   - Mobile-optimized

5. **Bidding Strategy**:
   - Start with manual CPC for learning
   - Transition to Target CPA after data collection
   - Adjust bids by device, location, time
   - Increase bids for high-converting keywords
   - Decrease bids for low-quality traffic

**Tactics**:
- Start with small budget to test (¥500K/month)
- A/B test ad copy and landing pages
- Use ad scheduling (higher bids during peak hours)
- Implement conversion tracking and attribution
- Create remarketing audiences
- Test responsive search ads
- Monitor search terms and add negatives
- Optimize for mobile (60%+ of traffic)

**Success Metrics**:
- Click-through rate (target: 5%+)
- Conversion rate (target: 7%+)
- Cost per acquisition (target: under ¥10,000)
- Quality Score (target: 7+)
- Return on ad spend (ROAS) (target: 8:1+)
- Impression share (target: 50%+)

**Budget Allocation**: ¥6 million (30% of Online-Families budget)
