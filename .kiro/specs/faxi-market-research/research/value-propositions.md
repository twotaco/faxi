## 1. Value Proposition: Elder-Offline-Fax-Users

### Core Promise

**"Access the digital world through your familiar fax machine—no new technology to learn, no burden on your family, maintain your independence with dignity."**

### Key Benefits

#### 1.1 Zero Learning Curve - Use What You Already Know

**Benefit**: Use your existing fax machine that you already know how to operate. No smartphones, no computers, no apps to download, no passwords to remember. Just send a fax like you always have, and Faxi handles the rest.

**Why This Matters**:
- 76% of elderly report anxiety about using new technology (34.2% very anxious, 41.8% somewhat anxious)
- 40.5% of elderly (14.9 million people) have low or no internet usage
- 38.7% of elderly households (14.2 million) already own fax machines
- 8.6 million elderly use fax regularly (weekly or more)

**How It Works**:
- Send a fax with your request (shopping list, appointment request, service inquiry)
- Faxi's AI interprets your handwritten or typed message
- Faxi accesses the online service on your behalf
- You receive a confirmation fax with results
- No learning, no training, no frustration

**Source**: Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications, "Communications Usage Trend Survey 2023", August 2023; Cabinet Office, "Survey on Attitudes of the Elderly 2023", March 2023


#### 1.2 Maintain Independence - Reduce Reliance on Children

**Benefit**: Shop online, book appointments, access government services, and more—all without relying on your children for help. Reduce the feeling of being a burden while staying connected to modern services.

**Why This Matters**:
- 59.5% of elderly live alone or with spouse only (not with children)
- 50% have children living more than 15 minutes away
- 54.2% of technology decisions require family involvement
- 54.6% of family caregivers feel guilty about not doing enough
- Strong cultural value of independence (自立, jiritsu) and not burdening others

**How It Works**:
- Make your own shopping orders without calling your children
- Book your own medical appointments without family help
- Access services on your own schedule, not when children are available
- Maintain control over your personal affairs and privacy
- Reduce frequency of "can you help me with..." phone calls

**Source**: National Institute of Population and Social Security Research, "Household Projections for Japan 2023", April 2023; Cabinet Office, "Survey on Family Caregiving Decision-Making 2022", December 2022

#### 1.3 Preserve Dignity and Self-Sufficiency

**Benefit**: Continue managing your own affairs with confidence. No need to admit you can't use technology or ask for help with every online task. Stay in control of your daily life.

**Why This Matters**:
- Dignity (尊厳, songen) is a core value for Japanese elderly
- 42.3% make care decisions independently when cognitively able
- 58.3% manage finances independently
- 48.7% downplay health issues to avoid worrying children
- Fear of losing independence is a primary concern

**How It Works**:
- Maintain privacy in your transactions (children don't see everything)
- Make decisions on your own timeline
- Avoid feeling "less capable" because you don't use smartphones
- Preserve your sense of competence and self-worth
- Continue living independently in your own home

**Source**: Cabinet Office, "Survey on Attitudes of the Elderly 2023", March 2023; Bank of Japan, "Survey on Household Financial Behavior 2023", June 2023

#### 1.4 Bridge to Family Connection

**Benefit**: Enable better communication with family members who use digital tools, while you use the fax machine you trust. Reduce worry for your children while maintaining your autonomy.

**Why This Matters**:
- 82.4% of elderly receive emotional support from family
- 67.2% communicate with children via phone weekly or more
- 38.4% of elderly wish for more frequent communication
- 78.3% of adult children believe they should care for parents
- Family harmony (和, wa) is culturally important

**How It Works**:
- Children can see you're successfully managing online services (reduces their worry)
- Less need for emergency "help me" calls
- Family can focus on quality time together, not tech support
- Demonstrate to family that you're staying engaged with modern world
- Maintain connection without dependency

**Source**: Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications, "Survey on Family Communication Patterns 2023", August 2023; Cabinet Office, "International Survey on Attitudes Toward Aging 2023", May 2023

#### 1.5 Safe and Secure - Protected from Online Risks

**Benefit**: No risk of online scams, phishing, or fraud. Faxi acts as a protective layer between you and the internet. Your personal information stays safe, and you get human support whenever you need it.

**Why This Matters**:
- 58.9% prioritize safety/security in technology decisions
- 38.7% have security concerns about online services
- 27.6% have privacy concerns about technology
- Elderly are frequent targets of online scams
- Trust is essential for adoption

**How It Works**:
- Never enter credit card information on websites
- No passwords to remember or accounts to manage
- Faxi verifies legitimate services and protects against fraud
- Human review of suspicious transactions
- Japanese language phone support available anytime
- No exposure to phishing emails or malicious websites

**Source**: Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications, "Survey on ICT Usage Among Elderly 2023", August 2023

**Benefit**: Priced at ¥980-1,980/month (less than ¥66/day), comparable to a few cups of coffee. Clear pricing with no hidden fees, no long-term contracts, cancel anytime.

**Why This Matters**:

- 64% have income ¥2-6 million (middle income)

## 2. Value Proposition: Online-Families

### Core Promise

**"Give your parents independence and yourself peace of mind—enable them to access modern services through their familiar fax machine, reducing your caregiving burden while maintaining their dignity."**

### Key Benefits

#### 2.1 Reduce Geographic Distance Barriers

**Benefit**: Enable your parents to access online services without your physical presence. No more rushing over to help with appointments or shopping, no matter how far away you live.

**Why This Matters**:
- 50% of adult children live more than 15 minutes from elderly parents
- 27.6% live in different prefectures (1+ hours away)
- 48.7% cite geographic distance as barrier to helping parents
- 45% of urban elderly have children living more than 1 hour away
- Migration pattern: children in cities, parents in rural/suburban areas

**How It Works**:
- Parents can shop online, book appointments, access services independently
- You don't need to visit or be available for every request
- Parents maintain independence despite distance
- You can monitor their activity (with permission) for peace of mind
- Reduces need for frequent "help me with..." phone calls

**Source**: Cabinet Office, "Survey on Attitudes of the Elderly 2023", March 2023; NTT Data Institute, "Survey on Intergenerational Technology Support 2022", October 2022

#### 2.2 Bridge the Technology Gap Without Teaching

**Benefit**: Your parents use their familiar fax machine while you benefit from digital integration. No more frustrating attempts to teach them smartphones or computers.

**Why This Matters**:
- 38.4% of adult children struggle with parents' inability to use digital tools
- 42.3% experience difficulty explaining technical concepts
- 38.2% cite parents' resistance to learning as barrier
- 24.6% admit lack of patience when teaching
- 31.7% find it frustrating when parents forget instructions
- Digital literacy programs have 40-60% dropout rates

**How It Works**:
- Parents use fax (which they already know)
- You don't need to teach, explain, or troubleshoot
- No repeated questions about "how do I do this again?"
- No guilt about not having time to teach
- Parents feel competent, you feel relieved

**Source**: NTT Data Institute, "Survey on Intergenerational Technology Support 2022", October 2022; Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications, "Digital Inclusion Initiative Progress Report 2023", October 2023

#### 2.3 Peace of Mind and Reduced Worry

**Benefit**: Know that your parents can access essential services (healthcare, shopping, emergencies) even when you're not available. Reduce constant worry about their wellbeing.

**Why This Matters**:
- 67.3% of family caregivers report emotional stress
- 54.6% feel guilty about not doing enough
- 63.4% lack personal time due to caregiving
- 38.4% of elderly parents wish for more frequent communication
- 42.7% of adult children feel parents expect too frequent contact
- Caregiving burden affects work-life balance for 51.2%

**How It Works**:
- Parents can handle daily needs independently
- You receive notifications of important activities (with permission)
- Emergency services accessible even when you're unavailable
- Reduced anxiety about parents being "cut off" from services
- Better work-life balance with less caregiving interruption

**Source**: Japan Institute for Labour Policy and Training, "Survey on Work-Life Balance and Caregiving 2022", November 2022; Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare, "Employment and Caregiving Survey 2022", September 2022

**Why This Matters**:

## 3. Value Proposition: Business Partners

### Core Promise

#### 3.1 Access Massive Untapped Market

**Benefit**: Reach 6-7.5 million elderly customers who currently cannot use your online services. Expand your addressable market by 40.5% of the elderly population.

**Why This Matters**:

**Healthcare Providers**:
- 62.3% of hospital admissions are elderly patients
- 54.7% of outpatient visits are elderly patients
- Only 34.2% of hospitals have online appointment systems
- Only 12.3% of elderly use online appointments
- 8.7% no-show rate due to communication barriers
- Missed appointments cost ¥15,000-30,000 each

**E-Commerce Platforms**:
- ¥22.7 trillion total e-commerce market
- Only 32.7% of elderly shop online
- 67.3% of elderly (24.7 million) don't use online shopping
- Potential ¥9.2 trillion market if elderly participated at same rate
- Elderly have ¥3.18 million average household income
- 64% have income ¥2-6 million (middle income with spending power)

**Government Services**:
- 94.2% of elderly use healthcare/insurance services
- 87.3% use pension services
- Only 18.7% use online government services
- 40.5% have low/no internet access
- Digital transformation mandate: all services online by 2025
- Accessibility compliance increasingly required

**Source**: Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare, "Patient Survey 2023", August 2023; Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry, "E-Commerce Market Survey 2023", August 2023; Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications, "Survey on Administrative Services Usage 2023", July 2023

#### 3.2 Reduce Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Benefit**: Acquire elderly customers at lower cost than traditional marketing channels. Direct access through familiar interface reduces friction and improves conversion.

**Why This Matters**:

**Current CAC for Elderly**:

## Implementation Recommendations

### Messaging Strategy

**Elder-Offline-Fax-Users**:
- Lead with familiarity and ease ("use your fax machine")
- Emphasize independence and dignity
- Use peer testimonials and social proof
- Simple, clear language with visual demonstrations
- Respectful tone that empowers rather than patronizes

**Online-Families**:
- Lead with burden reduction and peace of mind
- Emphasize time savings and work-life balance
- Use family testimonials showing both perspectives
- Practical, solution-focused language
- Empathetic tone acknowledging caregiving challenges

**Business Partners**:
- Lead with market opportunity and ROI
- Emphasize competitive advantage and differentiation
- Use data, case studies, and financial projections
- Professional, results-oriented language
- Confident tone demonstrating expertise

### Channel Strategy

**Elder-Offline-Fax-Users**:
- Traditional media (TV, newspapers, radio)
- Community centers and senior centers
- Healthcare provider recommendations
- Direct mail and flyers
- Word-of-mouth and peer referrals

**Online-Families**:
- Digital advertising (search, social media)
- Content marketing (blogs, articles)
- Eldercare platforms and forums
- Healthcare and community partnerships
- Family-focused media

**Business Partners**:
- Industry conferences and trade shows
- Direct sales and business development
- Trade publications and industry media
- Professional networks and associations
- Case studies and white papers

### Pricing Strategy

**Elder-Offline-Fax-Users**:
- B2C subscription: ¥980-1,980/month
- Clear, simple pricing with no hidden fees
- Family payment options available
- Free trial period to reduce risk

**Online-Families**:
- Same B2C pricing (¥980-1,980/month)
- Family plan options for multiple elderly relatives
- Gift subscription options
- Emphasize value vs. alternatives (cheaper than smartphone plans)

**Business Partners**:
- Healthcare: ¥50,000-200,000/month + ¥500-1,000/transaction
- E-commerce: ¥100,000-500,000/month + 3-5% transaction fee
- Government: ¥200,000-500,000/month + ¥300-500/transaction
- Integration fees: ¥2-30 million one-time
- ROI-based pricing to align incentives
